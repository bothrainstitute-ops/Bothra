🔬 Interactive Lab
0°C ICE ❄️
How it works: Drag the slider to add heat. Watch particles change from a rigid lattice (Solid/Ice) → flowing liquid (Water) → freely flying gas (Steam). This is the Kinetic Theory of Matter in action!
100°C STEAM 💨
Cooling Process: Drag left to cool the gas. Steam → Condensation (gas to liquid) → Freezing (liquid to solid). Particles slow down and come closer together as energy is removed.
-10°C
ICE ❄️
Click "Apply Heat" to raise the temperature.
Key Points: Temperature pauses at 0°C (Melting Point) while ice converts to water, then again at 100°C (Boiling Point) while water converts to steam. The energy goes into changing state, NOT raising temperature.
Click "Start Graph" to see Latent Heat plateaus in real time.
Latent Heat: The flat sections of the graph show that temperature does NOT change during a phase change. Energy called Latent Heat of Fusion (0°C) and Latent Heat of Vaporization (100°C) is absorbed without changing temperature.

Sublimation: Dry Ice (solid CO₂) turns directly into gas without becoming a liquid!
